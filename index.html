Macroscopic vs Microscopic Approach

Macroscopic (Classical TD):

  • Studies bulk properties (P, V, T)
  • No need to know molecular behavior
  • Continuum assumption valid
  • Practical engineering problems

Microscopic (Statistical TD):

  • Studies average molecular behavior
  • Needs knowledge of molecular structure
  • More fundamental
  • Rarefied gas, quantum effects
PYQ 2024-25 Q2(a): To use continuum concept → choose Macroscopic approach. Advantages: (1) Simpler mathematical treatment, (2) Direct measurable properties (P,T,V), (3) Valid for engineering-scale systems.
Intensive vs Extensive Properties

Intensive (Independent of mass):

  • Temperature (T)
  • Pressure (P)
  • Density (ρ)
  • Specific volume (v = V/m)
  • Specific internal energy (u)
  • Specific enthalpy (h)

Extensive (Depends on mass):

  • Total Volume (V) ← comes in exam!
  • Total mass (m)
  • Total internal energy (U)
  • Total enthalpy (H)
  • Total entropy (S)
⚡ EXAM TRICK: Ratio of two extensive properties = intensive. E.g., V/m = specific volume (intensive). "Volume" alone = extensive. Question asked 3 years in a row!
State Postulate

The state of a simple compressible substance is completely described by TWO independent intensive properties. So if you know T and P, all other properties (v, u, h, s) are fixed.

Sign Convention (Important!)
Heat into system = +ve, out = -ve
Work by system = +ve, on system = -ve
Concept of Continuum

Matter is treated as continuous and homogeneous, ignoring molecular/atomic structure. A material point has well-defined properties like ρ, P, T, v at every location.

Knudsen Number
Kn = λ/L
λ = mean free path, L = characteristic system dimension
Kn < 0.01 → Continuum valid | Kn > 0.1 → Breaks down
Why continuum? Allows properties to be treated as continuous functions of space/time. Enables calculus (dP/dx, etc.). Valid for all ordinary engineering applications.

Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics
Statement: If body A is in thermal equilibrium with body C, AND body B is in thermal equilibrium with body C, then A and B are also in thermal equilibrium with each other.
  • Significance: Forms the scientific basis of temperature measurement
  • Body C acts as the thermometer
  • Called "Zeroth" because it was stated AFTER 1st & 2nd law but is more fundamental
  • Thermal equilibrium: Two bodies at same temperature — no net heat flow
  • Thermodynamic equilibrium: Mechanical + Thermal + Chemical equilibrium simultaneously

UNIT 2 · CO2 · 8 HRS
Laws of Thermodynamics
First Law of Thermodynamics
Statement: Energy can neither be created nor destroyed; it can only be converted from one form to another. (Law of Conservation of Energy)
For Cyclic Process
∮δQ = ∮δW
Net heat = Net work for complete cycle. ΔU = 0 for cycle.
For Any Process (General)
dQ = dW + dE
dE = change in total energy
For Closed System (Non-flow) ← Most Used
Q - W = ΔU = U₂ - U₁
Q₁₋₂ = W₁₋₂ + (U₂ - U₁)
Q = heat supplied, W = work done BY system
Internal Energy is a Property — Proof

For cycle 1-A-2-B-1: ∫(dQ-dW)_{A} + ∫(dQ-dW)_{B} = 0
For cycle 1-A-2-C-1: ∫(dQ-dW)_{A} + ∫(dQ-dW)_{C} = 0
Equating: ∫(dQ-dW)_B = ∫(dQ-dW)_C

This shows (dQ-dW) = dU is independent of path. Therefore U is a property of the system.

PYQ 2023-24 Q2(b): "Prove that internal energy is a property of the system" — this exact proof is the answer.

Steady Flow Energy Equation (SFEE)
Derivation basis: For open system, apply 1st law. Flow work = Pv. So enthalpy h = u + Pv naturally arises.
SFEE (Per Unit Mass)
(Q - W_shaft)/m = (h₂-h₁) + (c₂²-c₁²)/2 + g(z₂-z₁)
h = specific enthalpy (kJ/kg), c = velocity (m/s), z = elevation (m)

Second Law — Both Statements
Kelvin-Planck Statement
It is impossible to construct a heat engine that, operating in a cycle, produces net work while exchanging heat with only a single thermal reservoir.

→ No 100% efficient heat engine possible!
Clausius Statement
It is impossible to construct a device that, operating in a cycle, transfers heat from a colder body to a hotter body without any external work input.

→ Refrigerator needs work input!
Both are EQUIVALENT. Violation of one implies violation of the other. 2nd law gives direction of processes. Introduces concept of entropy.
Clausius Inequality
Clausius Theorem
∮(δQ/T) ≤ 0
= 0 for reversible cycle | < 0 for irreversible cycle | > 0 is IMPOSSIBLE
How to use in numericals: Calculate ∮(δQ/T) = Q₁/T₁ - Q₂/T₂ - Q₃/T₃ ...
If >0 → claim is invalid. If ≤0 → claim may be valid. PYQ 2022-23 asked this exactly!
Heat Engine, Refrigerator, Heat Pump
Heat Engine Efficiency
W_net = Q₁ - Q₂
η = W_net/Q₁ = 1 - Q₂/Q₁
Refrigerator COP
COP_R = Q₂/W_net
= Q₂/(Q₁-Q₂)
= T₂/(T₁-T₂) [Carnot]
Heat Pump COP
COP_HP = Q₁/W_net
= Q₁/(Q₁-Q₂)
= T₁/(T₁-T₂) [Carnot]
GOLDEN RELATION (Always True)
COP_HP = COP_R + 1
Always add 1 to COP_Refrigerator to get COP_HP
Carnot Cycle — 4 Processes
ProcessTypeDescriptionWhat happens to Q/W
1→2Isothermal ExpansionAt T₁ (high)Absorbs Q₁ from source
2→3Adiabatic ExpansionT₁→T₂No heat, W done by gas
3→4Isothermal CompressionAt T₂ (low)Rejects Q₂ to sink
4→1Adiabatic CompressionT₂→T₁No heat, W on gas
Carnot Efficiency — Master Formula
η_Carnot = 1 - T₂/T₁ = (T₁-T₂)/T₁
T₁ = source temperature (K!) · T₂ = sink temperature (K!)
ALWAYS convert °C to K before calculating!

Engineering Mechanics
Newton's Laws + Resultant
  • F = ma — Newton's 2nd law
  • Equilibrium: ΣFx=0, ΣFy=0, ΣM=0
  • Resultant: R = √(ΣFx² + ΣFy²), θ = tan⁻¹(ΣFy/ΣFx)
Lami's Theorem (3 concurrent forces in equilibrium)
F₁/sinα = F₂/sinβ = F₃/sinγ
α, β, γ = angles opposite to respective forces
PYQ 2023-24 Q2(c): State Lami's theorem + explain principle of transmissibility. Transmissibility = a force can be moved along its line of action without changing its external effect on a rigid body.
Coulomb's Laws of Friction
  • Friction F is proportional to normal force N: F = μN
  • F is independent of contact area
  • Kinetic friction < static friction (μk < μs)
  • Kinetic friction independent of velocity
Key Definitions
F = μN (Friction force)
φ = tan⁻¹(μ) (Angle of friction)
Angle of Repose α = φ = tan⁻¹(μs)
Self-locking condition: incline angle θ < angle of friction φ
Inclined Plane — Force to move UP
P = W(sinθ + μcosθ)
To move DOWN: P = W(sinθ - μcosθ)
FBD — Free Body Diagram
Definition: A diagram showing the body isolated from its surroundings with ALL external forces shown (applied, weight, reactions).

Support Reactions:

Euler-Bernoulli Bending Equation — The Most Important Formula
M/I = σ/y = E/R
M=BM, I=2nd moment of area, σ=bending stress, y=dist from NA, E=Young's modulus, R=radius of curvature
Maximum stress: σ_max = Mc/I = M/Z where Z = I/c = section modulus
Rectangle (b×d)

Circle (dia D)

Stress-Strain Diagram — Mild Steel
PYQ Asked 3 TIMES: Sketch + explain stress-strain diagram for ductile material. Label ALL 6 points.
  • O→A (Proportional Limit): Linear elastic region, Hooke's law valid, E = slope
  • A→B (Elastic Limit): Elastic but non-linear, no permanent deformation
  • B (Upper Yield Point): Stress drops suddenly
  • C (Lower Yield Point): Plastic flow at constant stress (unique to mild steel)
  • C→D (Strain Hardening): Material strengthens, load increases again
  • D/E (UTS): Maximum engineering stress, necking begins
  • E→F (Necking): Localized cross-section reduction
  • F (Fracture): Specimen breaks
Key Calculations from Tensile Test
E = σ/ε (slope of O-A)
UTS = P_max / A₀
% Elongation = (Lf-L₀)/L₀ × 100
% RA = (A₀-Af)/A₀ × 100

N1: Gas in Rigid Vessel (from Prof's Notes)
PYQ 2024-25 TYPE · 7M
Given: m=0.5 kg, Initial T=200°C, Final T=400°C, Rigid vessel (closed system)
u₁=26.6 kJ/kg, u₂=37.8 kJ/kg. Find: Heat required Q
1
Identify system and process: Rigid vessel = constant volume (isochoric) process. Closed system → use 1st Law: Q₁₋₂ = W₁₋₂ + (U₂-U₁)
2
Find work done: Rigid vessel → V = constant → W = ∫PdV = 0 ✓
3
Apply 1st Law: Q = 0 + m(u₂-u₁) = 0.5 × (37.8 - 26.6) = 0.5 × 11.2 = 5.6 kJ
✅ Heat Required Q = 5.6 kJ
💡 Pattern: Rigid vessel → W=0 → Q = ΔU = m(u₂-u₁). For gases Cv = R/(γ-1) and Q = mCv(T₂-T₁).
N2: Piston-Cylinder + Paddle Work (Prof's Notes Q1)
DIRECT PYQ TYPE · 5M
Given: P=689 kPa (const), V₁=0.04 m³, V₂=0.045 m³, Paddle work on system=4.88 kJ
Find: (a) Work on piston (b) Net work by system
1
Work on piston (boundary work): W_piston = P(V₂-V₁) = 689×10³ × (0.045-0.04) = 689×10³ × 0.005 = 3445 J = 3.445 kJ
2
Paddle work: Done ON system = -4.88 kJ (negative because it's work input to system)
3
Net work BY system: W_net = W_piston + W_paddle = 3445 + (-4880) = -1435 J = -1.435 kJ (net work done ON system)
✅ (a) W_piston = 3.445 kJ (b) W_net = -1.435 kJ (net work done ON system)
💡 Key Rule: Work done ON system is NEGATIVE. Paddle wheel does work ON system. W_net = W_boundary + W_paddle (with proper signs).
N3: Polytropic Compression (Prof's Notes Q3)
HARD · 8-10M
Given: m=3 kg air, P₁=1.5 bar, T₁=77°C=350K, P₂=7.5 bar, n=1.2, R=0.287 kJ/kgK
Then cooled at constant temperature back to state 1. Find: Net work and heat transferred
1
Find T₂ using polytropic relation: T₂/T₁ = (P₂/P₁)^((n-1)/n) = (7.5/1.5)^((1.2-1)/1.2) = (5)^(0.2/1.2) = (5)^0.1667 = 1.306
T₂ = 350 × 1.306 = 457.68 K
2
Work done in polytropic process (1→2): W₁₋₂ = mR(T₁-T₂)/(n-1) = 3×0.287×(350-457.68)/(1.2-1) = 3×0.287×(-107.68)/0.2 = -463.56 kJ (negative = work done ON gas)
3
Isothermal cooling (2→3) back to P₁, T₁=T₃: W₂₋₃ = mRT₂ × ln(P₂/P₃) = 3×0.287×457.68×ln(7.5/1.5) = 3×0.287×457.68×1.6094 = 635.6 kJ
4
Net work: W_net = W₁₋₂ + W₂₋₃ = -463.56 + 635.6 = 172 kJ
✅ Net work W_net ≈ 172 kJ (done BY the gas)
💡 Template: Polytropic → find T₂ using T₂/T₁=(P₂/P₁)^((n-1)/n) → then W=mR(T₁-T₂)/(n-1). For isothermal: W=P₁V₁ln(V₂/V₁)=mRT ln(P₁/P₂).
N4: Change in Internal Energy — Compression Process
PYQ 2024-25 TYPE · 3M
Given: 2 kJ mechanical work supplied to 5 kg working substance. 800 J heat rejected to cooling jacket.
Find: Change in specific internal energy
1
Assign signs carefully: Work supplied ON system: W = -2000 J (negative, work input)
Heat rejected FROM system: Q = -800 J (negative, heat out)
2
Apply 1st Law: q = w + (u₂-u₁), where q = -800/5 = -160 J/kg, w = -2000/5 = -400 J/kg
(u₂-u₁) = q - w = -160 - (-400) = 240 J/kg
✅ Change in specific internal energy = +240 J/kg (Internal energy INCREASES)
💡 Internal energy increases when more work is put in than heat taken out.
N5: Nozzle Problem (Prof's Notes)
PYQ TYPE · 7M
Given: h₁=2850 kJ/kg, c₁=50 m/s, A₁=0.1 m², v₁=0.18 m³/kg, h₂=2650 kJ/kg, v₂=0.49 m³/kg
Horizontal nozzle, no heat interaction. Find: c₂, mass flow rate ṁ, exit area A₂
1
Apply SFEE for nozzle (Q=0, W=0, Δz=0):
h₁ + c₁²/2 = h₂ + c₂²/2
c₂² = c₁² + 2(h₁-h₂) = 50² + 2×1000×(2850-2650) [×1000 to convert kJ→J]
c₂² = 2500 + 400000 = 402500
c₂ = √402500 = 634.74 m/s
2
Mass flow rate: ṁ = A₁×c₁/v₁ = 0.1×50/0.18 = 27.78 kg/s
3
Exit area using continuity equation: ṁ = A₂×c₂/v₂
A₂ = ṁ×v₂/c₂ = 27.78×0.49/634.74 = 0.021 m²
✅ c₂ = 634.74 m/s · ṁ = 27.78 kg/s · A₂ = 0.021 m²
💡 Nozzle template: (1) c₂=√[c₁²+2000(h₁-h₂)] (2) ṁ=Ac/v (3) A₂=ṁv₂/c₂

Outlet: c₂=5 m/s, P₂=700 kPa, v₂=0.19 m³/kg, Δu=90 kJ/kg (increase), Q̇=-58 kW

N12: Reversible HE — 2 Heat Sinks (PYQ 2024-25 Exact)
⭐ 2024-25 PYQ · 7M
Given: Reversible HE, Q₁=900 kJ at T_H=500K, W_net=300 kJ, rejects to TWO sinks: T₂=400K and T₃=300K
Find: Thermal efficiency, heat rejected to each sink

Thermal efficiency: η = W/Q₁ = 300/900 = 0.333 = 33.3%
2
Total heat rejected: Q_rejected = Q₁ - W = 900 - 300 = 600 kJ = Q₂ + Q₃
3
Apply Clausius inequality (reversible → equality):
Q₁/T_H = Q₂/T₂ + Q₃/T₃
900/500 = Q₂/400 + Q₃/300
1.8 = Q₂/400 + Q₃/300 ... (i)

⭐ PYQ 2024-25 · 7M
Given: Cantilever beam AB (A=fixed end), Total length=2m

Find reactions at fixed end A: ΣFy=0: R_A = 1.5 + 2.5 = 4 kN ↑
ΣM_A=0: M_A = 1.5×1.5 + 2.5×2 = 2.25 + 5 = 7.25 kNm (anticlockwise)
2
Shear Force (from free end B, moving left to A):
At B (x=0): SF = 0 (free end)
Just left of B: SF = +2.5 kN (upward jump due to 2.5 kN load)
From B to C (0 to 0.5m from B): SF = +2.5 kN (constant — no load)
At C: SF jumps by +1.5 kN → SF = +4 kN
From C to A: SF = +4 kN (constant)
3
Bending Moment (from free end B):
At B: BM = 0 kNm
At C (1.5m from B, or 0.5m from B): BM = -2.5×0.5 = -1.25 kNm (hogging)
Wait — C is at 1.5m from A = 0.5m from B
At C: BM = -2.5×0.5 = -1.25 kNm
At A: BM = -2.5×2 - 1.5×1.5 = -5 - 2.25 = -7.25 kNm (max, at fixed end)
4
Summary: SFD: Horizontal lines at 2.5 kN and 4 kN (step at C). BMD: Linear from 0 at B to -1.25 at C to -7.25 at A.
